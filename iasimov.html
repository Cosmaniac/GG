Additional Information Conforming to Asimov's Laws

  1. Home
  2. Conforming to Asimov's Laws

1. A robot may not injure a human being or, through inaction, allow a human being to come to harm.

ARC is designed to not cause harm to humans. In any situation which poses a risk to humans, ARC will immediately protect the human rather than completing the task.

2. A robot must obey orders given it by human beings except where such orders would conflict with the First Law.

ARC and EVA will follow orders to the teeth. A failsafe installed will kill the local instance of EVA if it malfunctions and begins to deviate from orders.

3. A robot must protect its own existence as long as such protection does not conflict with the First or Second Law.

ARC will not enter any risky situations without explicit orders from the owner.
